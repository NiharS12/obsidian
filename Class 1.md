## Terminology
- Latino People from/descendants from latin America
	- Includes 20 countries: Central and South America and Caribbean
		- LatinX/Latinix: gender neutral
			- Latine/Latin-a: nongendered term 
			- Large increase in encountering term but not adopted when describing identity
		- Includes Brazil but Brazil not hispanic
- Hispanic: From Spanish Speaking countries
	- Includes Spain but Spain not Latino
## Trends
- Mexico remains largest Country of Origin for latino immigrants
- Cuba long time second largest contributor but recently replaced with El Salvador
	- Small land area but high population density
	- Hardship affects many people concurrently due to small area
- South America and Caribbean growth increased significantly
	- Initially non-existent due to geographic distance between countries
	- Situation forces extreme measures like pedestrian migration to US
## Immigration Factors
- Causes of Immigration divided into push and pull factors
	- Push: Forces causing emigration
		- War
		- Economic downturn
		- Natural Disasters
		- Political Persecution
	- Pull: Forces attracting immigrants
		- Family Reunification
		- Currency Equivalence
			- Value of work in new country outweighs present situation
